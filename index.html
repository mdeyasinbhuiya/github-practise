Cox’s Bazar –
The Longest Sea Beach

Cox's Bazar is the world’s longest natural sandy sea beach, stretching about 120 km along the southeastern coastline of Bangladesh. Beyond its scenic beauty, here are some lesser-known facts:

  1. Name Origin: It’s named after Captain Hiram Cox, a British officer known for resolving conflicts among local communities.
  2. Inani Beach: Famous for coral stones that shimmer under the sun.
  3. Laboni Point: The most popular beach section for tourists.
  4. Marine Drive: The world’s longest sea-view road, running 80 km
  5. Saint Martin’s Island: The only coral island in Bangladesh, accessible from Teknaf.

Bandarban –
The Hill Tracts

Bandarban, a picturesque district in the Chittagong Hill Tracts of Bangladesh, is known for its serene hills and rich tribal culture. Some lesser-known facts include:

  1. Highest Peaks: It is home to the country’s highest peaks, including Tahjindong (1,280m), Keokradong (1,230m), and Saka Haphong (potentially higher).
  2. Diverse Tribes: Eleven indigenous groups, including Marma, Tripura, and Mro, live here.
  3. Unique Caves: The mysterious Boga Lake has underwater caves and legends surrounding its origin.
  4. Golden Temple: The largest Theravada Buddhist temple in Bangladesh
  5. No Foreigners Until 1990s:Previously restricted due to security reasons

Sylhet –
The Tea Capital

Sylhet, located in northeastern Bangladesh, is renowned for its lush tea gardens and vibrant landscapes. Here are some lesser-known facts:

  1. Tea Capital: Sylhet produces the majority of Bangladesh’s tea, with over 150 tea estates, including the famous Malnichhera, the oldest tea garden in the country.
  2. Stone Collections: Jaflong is known for its scenic beauty and stone collection industry.
  3. Spiritual Heritage: It houses Hazrat Shah Jalal's Shrine, attracting thousands of pilgrims.
  4. Ratargul Swamp Forest: The only freshwater swamp forest in Bangladesh.
  5. Rich Diaspora: A large Sylheti population resides in the UK, especially in London.

Kuakata –
The Sea Beach with Sunrise and Sunset Views

Kuakata, known as the "Daughter of the Sea", is a unique sea beach in southern Bangladesh. Here are some lesser-known facts:

  1. Sunrise and Sunset View: It’s one of the few beaches in the world where both sunrise and sunset can be observed from the same spot.
  2. Origin of the Name: The name "Kuakata" comes from the word "Kua" (well), referring to wells dug by early settlers for fresh water.
  3. Coconut Grove: Famous for its vast coconut plantations near the beach.
  4. Rakhine Tribe: Home to the indigenous Rakhine community
  5. Fatrar Char: A beautiful forested island accessible by boat.

Sajek Valley –
The Queen Of Hill

Sajek Valley, nestled in the Chittagong Hill Tracts, is often called the "Roof of Rangamati" for its high altitude and breathtaking views. Here are some lesser-known facts:

  1. Named After a River: It’s named after the Sajek River, flowing along the India-Bangladesh border.
  2. Mysterious Clouds: The valley is often enveloped in clouds, creating a surreal atmosphere.
  3. No Electricity Until 2017: Solar panels powered homes before electricity was introduced.
  4. Indigenous Communities: The Lushai, Chakma, and Tripura tribes live here.
  5. Konglak Hill: The highest peak offers panoramic views and is popular for sunrise treks.
